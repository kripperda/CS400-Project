Scenario 1 - Shannon

Shannon is looking for toys for Christmas in an initiative called "Thriftmas" which is where gently used toys are bought second hand rather than spending a lot of money on new toys for Christmas presents. She would rather save for things her children need such as books and clothes rather than toys they will play with for a few months then forget about.

Shannon begins asking on social media platforms for gently used toys as well as searching sites like Marketplace and Mercari for cheap or free toys. She has discovered that everything requires batteries. Most of the toys found are in good shape, but the batteries have died and the children stoped playing with it.

After inquiring about the toys, people stopped responding as regularly. Her regular question about battery level appears to be the stopper in her inquiries. Shannon wishes there was a program or site that would require sellers or offerors to make sure the batteries are in working order or offer new batteries rather than just stop responding.



Scenario 2 - Shannon

Shannon has an 8 year old that is getting into more science-based activities. She has inquired with the library and rented out books for activities that require more ingredients and items than typical household items. These would be beneficial for Girl Scouts activities as well.

She seeks input from her network of friends and gathers quite a few of the items needed but with several more to go.

She has found there is a science store about 30 miles away that has many of these items to do multiple activities for cheap. However, this store is well out of the way. There are no times when she will head that way to get the remaining items.

Outside of her friend network she wishes there were a site for hand-me-down activities and items for these science experiments that sources the items based on location near to her. She could stop off on her way to work or the store to pick up on a porch, even for a small fee.



Scenario 3 - Shannon

With children of various ages, Shannon has been able to pass down toys and her younger children have become very good at playing with toys and puzzles beyond their current age. However, she is finding that there is a shortage among friends and family for puzzles of various difficulty.

Any puzzles handed down tend to be old and missing pieces. Or the pieces are bent and do not connect properly. This makes it difficult for her kids to solve and they get frustrated.

She goes to the store and looks online for new puzzles, but they are not a cheap activity. Any puzzles listed on Marketplace simply show the box and are listed for near retail prices.

She wishes the seller or any offering for free would require a picture of the complete puzzle in the listing to allevaite frustration for her children and also herself when they get frustrated.
