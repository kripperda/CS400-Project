Scenario 1 - Tracy

Tracy is a soon to be grandmother. Her son and daughter-in-law have had several baby showers to gather items they will need for their new baby. The primary items they are lacking are toys for newborns. This includes books and other stimulation toys for tummy time.

After searching stores, the number of toys became overwhelming to pick. She sought advice from her friends and other grandparents to determine what was actually needed. Her friends provided some great advice, but what she really needed was real world experience for certain items.

She was thinking of a collection of toys that she could try and pass down to other grandmothers or even mothers in need. Specifically, she was most worried about what would be most appropriate for a newborn as she anticipated babysitting the baby quite often.



Scenario 2 - Tracy

Tracy is looking for new toys for her grandchild. As a grandparent, the baby will not be at their home all the time so she does not want to break the bank on buying toys.

She has browsed websites and considered sites that donate toys rather than buying directly. However she cannot find anything nearby that would satisfy her need for limited toys for cheap or free.

Location is important as she cannot drive very far due to an eye condition. She would prefer something she could pick up in her neighborhood rather than driving across town.



Scenario 3 - Tracy

Tracy's children are grown and out of the house. For the past several years she has considered removing their old items that they used when they were younger, but she is not sure what to do with them. There is a lot of things in her basement that are taking up space.

She thinks about trying to sell, but that would be time consuming and burdensome for very little return. She would prefer to donate what she can in addition to throwing some items out that are no longer fit for use. Many of the items are still in good condition.

Resources for donations are lacking in her area. She is seeking an aggregated spot that shows what donation centers are in most need of what toys. She also believes it to be importatnt that the donation organization can place the items the hands of those in the most need.

She believes the best approach would be for all places taking donations should be listed in a single location and those places could, in turn, list the toys and other items for free so that those less fortunate can receive what they need.
